I'm Hrishikesh Bhaskaran. I work as the Vice President of Engineering at [Entri](https://entri.me), a Kochi-based ed-tech startup. I joined as a product engineer and grew with the company over the years. Now I spend most of my time deciding what to build, how to build it, and making sure the right people are working on the right problems.

A lot of that comes down to figuring out how to split teams so they match the systems they own. I think about build-vs-buy tradeoffs, treating technical debt as a portfolio rather than a backlog, and where AI actually helps versus where it just adds noise.

The goal is always the same: good engineering decisions should happen without me in the room.

Before the meetings took over, I was writing code full-time. I still do when I can.

## Free software & language computing

I've been around the free and open source software community for over a decade. Right now I contribute to the [PeARS Project](https://pearsproject.org/), a community effort to build a distributed search engine that respects user privacy. Decentralized systems, language computing, and privacy are what keep pulling me back.

I got my start through [Swathanthra Malayalam Computing](https://smc.org.in), a community that builds language computing tools for Malayalam: input methods, fonts, spell checkers, and more. Millions of people use these tools today. I was an active member and served as Secretary at one point. Through SMC I ended up working with organizations like Mozilla, Wikimedia, and ICFOSS on free and open source software adoption and digital literacy in Kerala.

## Screenwriting

I contributed the additional screenplay for the 2023 Malayalam film *[Neelavelicham](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neelavelicham_(film))*, directed by Aashiq Abu. An adaptation of Vaikom Muhammad Basheer's classic story that I grew up with.

## Other things

I was one of the architects of the [ICU movement](https://www.facebook.com/InternationalChaluUnion/), a socio-cultural expression platform of Kerala's youth.

I occasionally do college sessions and TV discussions on net neutrality, privacy, and free software.
